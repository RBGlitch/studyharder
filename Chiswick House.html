Chiswick House

Chiswick House is a small summer palace that was built in the 1720s by Lord Burlington in collaboration by architect William Kent. At the beginning of the 18th century, they were the initiators of the artistic movement in nNeoclassical architecture, which was called Palladianism. The villa belonged by Burlington to house his collection of antiques, not to live in, so there is no dining room or bedroom. Masonic symbols indicate that Masonic lodge meetings may have been held at Chiswick House. People said that Burlington was a member of the Freemasons. In 1813, a 96-meter greenhouse was built on the territory of the Chiswick estate. It is considered the largest in England, which is famous for its camellias. During its history, the villa has been a mental hospital, a fire station, and is now listed in English heritage.
