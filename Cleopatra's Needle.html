Cleopatra's Needle

Cleopatra's Needle in London is one of a pair of obelisks, together named Cleopatra's Needle's, that were relocated from the ruins of the Caesareum of Alexandria in the 19-th centrury. Inscribed by Thutmose III and Ramesses II of the Egyptian New Kingdom, the obelisk was moved to Alexandria in 12 BC, where it had remained for nearly two millenia It was originally planned to be installed in front of British Parlament building. On september 12, 1878, it was installed on the Vickoria Embankment. On the sides there are sculptures of sphinxes, cast in bronze and facing the obelisk
