Ham House

Ham House is a rich Jacobin mansion of the 17th century It is located on the banks of the Thames in Richmond. It was originally a country villa, but the owners turned it into a luxurious palace. In Ham House you can see the Grand Staircase, the Long Gallery and the Nothern Drawing Room. The house is complemented by ancient outbuildings: a glacire, an oil mill and the oldest greenhouse in the country. Also next to the building there is is a garden with amazing sculptures. More than 250 species of plants grow there. In summer, open-air concerts are held there.
